Memphis

Memphis, Tennessee, is a culturally rich city located along the Mississippi River, known for its historical
significance in music, particularly as the birthplace of blues and rock 'n' roll, and its pivotal role in the American Civil Rights Movement.

The City of Memphis

Chicago

Chicago is the third largest city in the United States, renowned for its vibrant cultural
scene, iconic architecture, and significant historical role as a transportation and economic hub in the Midwest.

City of Chicago

Baltimore

Baltimore, Maryland, is the state's largest city and a significant cultural and economic hub, known for its historic
waterfront, diverse neighborhoods, and pivotal role in American history, including its connection to the War of 1812 and the birthplace of the national anthem.

City of Baltimore

New York

New York City, often referred to as NYC, is the most populous city in the United States, known for its iconic skyline, diverse
cultural landscape, and status as a global center for finance, art, and entertainment, encompassing five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island.

City of New York

Los Angeles

Los Angeles, often abbreviated as L.A., is the largest city in California and the second most populous city in the
United States, recognized as a global cultural, financial, and commercial center, famed for its entertainment industry, diverse population, and Mediterranean climate.

City of Los Angeles
